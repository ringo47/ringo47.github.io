Stove Controller

Turn the heat up or down from your phone via Bluetooth/Wi-Fi

A one-day DIY build to control my stove with my phone via WiFi/Bluetooth. Designed and printed a servo mount that can just snap onto the regulator board. Glued the servo to the horn adapter so that it can turn the knob. Now I also can set it to turn off in whatever pattern I want it to. High for 5 mins, then simmer for 15 mins and turn off. Since it also has manual control, I can sit in my room working on my homework and just turn the stove off without moving an inch.

2D - Plotter

A simple retrofit to convert a 3D-printer to a 2D- Plotter.

A holder clipped on to the extruder to plot any G-Code onto anything. Useful to mark holes or pick up points accurately on parts. Can also hold a precision knife to cut an outline on a sheet or a cardboard. Image converted to svg in Inkscape and the path is exported to G-Code. G-Code can be calibrated using Repetier.

Composite Laminate Analyzer

A brute force script that analyzes all possible laminate combinations with a pre-defined increment to find the best possible ply combinations.

The task for the final project of my 'Mechanics of Composite materials' course was to design a 6-ply composite for the top spar cap of a wind-mill. After deciding on the loads, I decided to use our university's high performance computing grid to run around 5 million combination of plies and the and it resulted in a ply combination that was stronger than anyone else's by at-least a factor of 7. The .m files can be found here.

Spheron

An omni-directional autonomous spherical rolling robot.

Inspired by Goodyear's Eagle-360 and BB-8 from Star Wars, I decided to build an omni-directional autonomous spherical robot. Built the casing by laying fiber-glass and 2 layers of Carbon-Fibre over a 15-inch beach ball. The drive unit inside was coupled to the sensor unit on the top by Neodymium magnets and the communication was enabled via a radio transmitter-receiver unit. The sensor had two ultra-sonic ranging sensors which pings the distance to ground and will detect an obstacle of a given height(I put 5cm) and stop the drive unit inside via the radio. More details here.

Cone-Screw SuperCharger

An electric prototype supercharger.

A senior's idea to extract more power from our RE 500cc engine. A double helix wrapped around a cone with inlet and outlet at the opposite ends and the motor would be mounted to the head of the cone. Flow-Simulation resulted in 6psi boost at 1000rads/s and 0.75N-m of torque. Theoretically, this would boost the engine's naturally aspirated 25hp to ~32hp peak power.here.

3D files and the flow simulation results can be found here.
